Chapter 1

How Atlas works

Atlas is a three-tier system. Knowing where you sit in it makes everything else easier to navigate.

TIER 1

The Agency

Atlas itself. Andrew's team runs this. You don't see it.

TIER 2 · YOU

Your Business

Where you live. Set up offers, rewards, streaks, memberships. Run the front desk.

TIER 3

Your Customers

Members. They install your branded app. Scan QR codes. Earn points. Redeem rewards.

Your two roles

Atlas gives every business two surfaces, and you'll move between them:

  • Manager dashboard — the deep settings (rewards, offers, streaks, membership setup, billing, team, analytics). The "behind-the-scenes" cockpit.
  • Front desk app — the live transactional surface (scan QR, award points, fulfill redemptions, activate memberships, approve reviews). What you use during business hours.

Both live at the same URL — yourbusiness.atlas-engine.org/manage. The tabs along the top switch between them.

Mental model: Manager dashboard is the recipe book. Front desk is the kitchen.

What your customers see

Your customers visit yourbusiness.atlas-engine.org on their phone, sign up, and install the app to their home screen (one tap on Android; tap Share → Add to Home Screen on iPhone). From that moment on:

  • Your logo lives in their dock
  • They scan your QR code at the front desk to earn points
  • They get push notifications when you broadcast or their streak is about to break
  • They redeem rewards in-app
Chapter 2

First 30 minutes: setting up your business

Follow these in order. If a step takes more than 5 minutes, skip and come back.

1

Log in to your dashboard.

Your invite email has a link. Click it and set your password. Land on the dashboard.

2

Upload your logo + hero image.

Top of the business editor. Square PNG, at least 512×512 for the logo (this becomes the app icon on customer phones). The hero is a landscape photo of your space (about 1600×900).

3

Set your brand colors.

Pick a primary + secondary. The customer app's gradients, buttons, and accents all derive from these. Test by tapping the phone-preview panel on the right — updates instantly.

4

Write a welcome message.

One line, friendly. Default is "Welcome back, <first name>!" — fine to keep, or customize ("Hey there, hope your coffee was great ☕").

5

Set point rules.

How many points per visit, per dollar spent, per review submitted, per referral, per birthday. Defaults work — only revisit if you want to change the value of a redemption.

6

Add your first 3 rewards.

Click Rewards → Add reward. Suggested starter trio: a low-cost daily (e.g. "Free upgrade — 50 pts"), a mid-range (e.g. "Free drink — 200 pts"), and an aspirational (e.g. "VIP day pass — 1000 pts").

7

Print your QR code.

Click QR Code on the business header. Download the PNG. Print on cardstock and put it where customers can see + reach. Recommended size: at least 3×3 inches at eye level.

Pro tip: Tape an A5 mini-flyer next to the QR with two lines — "Scan to earn points. Today's bonus: +50 points for joining." That's your first-week sign-up engine.

Optional but recommended (next 30 minutes)

8

Turn on streaks.

Membership → Streaks → Enable. Set the cadence (daily, weekly, monthly) and add 2-3 milestone rewards. Default config works for most businesses.

9

Set up your Google review link.

Settings → Business Settings → Google Review URL. Grab the link from Google Business Profile → "Get more reviews." Paste it. Customers will now tap "Review on Google" in the app and land directly at the review form.

10

Configure your membership (optional).

Membership tab → set a name, price, perks. Pick your payment mode: front-desk, external link, or Stripe. See Chapter 6 for details.

11

Create your first featured offer.

Offers tab → Add offer → mark it as Featured. Shows in the customer app's sticky banner at the top of every tab.

12

Invite your team.

Team tab → Invite. Add manager-level for people who handle billing + insights, front-desk-level for people who just scan + award. They get a magic link email.

How to know you're ready to flip the sign

You're set up when these are all true:

  • Logo + colors look right in the phone preview
  • At least 3 rewards in the catalog
  • QR code printed and placed
  • You can sign in as a customer (use a personal email on a different device) and see your branded app

That's it. Tell your first customer.

Chapter 3

Rewards catalog

Rewards are what customers spend their points on. The whole loyalty loop hinges on having a catalog that feels worth the grind.

How to set up a reward

  1. Rewards tab → Add reward
  2. Name it (short — "Free coffee", "10% off any service")
  3. Set the point cost (more on this below)
  4. Upload an image (square, ~500×500 PNG/JPG — appears on the reward card)
  5. Optionally set an expiration window on the redemption (e.g. "use within 14 days")
  6. Save

The point-cost math

Two ways to think about pricing your rewards:

By visit count

If you give 10 points per visit, a reward worth a free $5 item should cost ~50-100 points (5-10 visits).

By dollar value

1 point ≈ $0.01 of value. So a $10 reward costs ~1000 points. Adjust to your margin.

Reward ladder strategy

A healthy catalog has three rungs:

  • Quick win (50-100 pts) — gets new members to redeem within their first 2 weeks. Small free upgrade, drink size up, etc.
  • Habit reward (200-500 pts) — the bread-and-butter. The thing they're saving for.
  • Aspirational (1000+ pts) — the trophy. Brings out the competitive types.
Don't make all rewards the same point value. A catalog where everything costs 200 points feels boring. Variety creates anticipation.
Chapter 4

Offers — one-time + automated + voice memos

Offers are limited-time deals you push to your customers. They're different from rewards (which are always available for points). Atlas has two types.

One-Time offers

You create them. You expire them. Used for promotions, slow days, holidays.

  1. Offers tab → One-Time → Add offer
  2. Title (short — "Free side with any entree")
  3. Description, image, expiration date
  4. Mark as Featured to show in the sticky banner on every customer-app tab
  5. Save → live immediately
Featured offer = your slow-day weapon. Change it weekly. Customers who get push notifications will check the banner first.

Automated offers

Atlas drops these automatically based on events:

  • Birthday — auto-issued on the customer's birthday
  • Inactivity / Reactivation — auto-triggered if a customer hasn't visited in N days
  • Milestone celebrations — first visit, 10th visit, etc.
  • Holiday templates — pre-built for major dates (Mother's Day, St. Patrick's, etc.)

How to set one up:

  1. Offers → Automated → Add automated offer
  2. Pick the trigger (birthday, inactivity, milestone, etc.)
  3. Choose discount type: % or $
  4. Upload an image (this is what the customer sees in the "OfferReveal" popup)
  5. Save → Atlas takes over the trigger

Voice memo offers

The killer feature. Record a 15-second audio message that plays in the customer's app when they receive the offer.

  1. When editing an automated offer, click the microphone icon
  2. Record (your voice, the owner's voice — feels personal)
  3. Preview, re-record if needed
  4. Save
What to say: "Hey Sarah, it's Joe from Joe's Cafe — noticed you haven't been in for a couple weeks. Coffee's on me next time. See you soon!" — that's it.

The OfferReveal popup

When a customer receives an automated offer, Atlas shows them a celebratory "unwrap" popup the next time they open the app. Confetti, slide-up reveal, voice memo plays if you recorded one. Builds anticipation.

Featured offer banner

The sticky orange banner that appears at the top of every customer-app tab. Always shows your current featured offer. To set:

  1. Edit any one-time offer
  2. Toggle Featured on (only one can be featured at a time)
  3. Save

Now appears across all tabs (Home, Scan, Rewards, Profile) until you remove it or it expires.

Customer offers broadcast

Sometimes you want to push a message right now to all members. Holiday hours, last-minute drop, surprise.

  1. Notifications tab → fill in title + body
  2. Pick where the notification opens to (Home / Rewards / Scan)
  3. Send → in-app notification + push notification to everyone who has push enabled
Don't abuse this. Once or twice a week max. People mute apps that spam.

Offer best practices

  • Use real images of your actual product, not stock photos
  • Keep titles to 4 words or fewer
  • Always set an expiration date — scarcity drives action
  • Test holiday templates a week in advance, not the day-of
Chapter 5

Streaks & milestone rewards

Streaks are how Atlas turns one-time visitors into habits. Members see their visit streak as a row of glowing fire cubes. Some cubes are milestone rewards — gold-plated, with a "★ REWARD" tag — and they're the thing customers come back for.

Configuring streaks

  1. Streaks tab in the manager dashboard
  2. Toggle Enable streaks on
  3. Choose cadence: daily (good for cafes, daily check-ins), weekly (most retail/service), or monthly (membership-style)
  4. Set check-ins required per period (default: 1)
  5. Add milestone rewards (see below)
  6. Save

Milestone rewards

The magic of streaks. Click Add milestone in the streak editor and set:

  • The visit count for the milestone (e.g. week 4, week 8, week 12)
  • The reward — bonus points, a free item, or a mystery reward (Atlas picks a random surprise)
  • The label customers see ("Week 4 reward 🎉")

The 4-8-12 framework

Tested milestone cadence that works for most local businesses:

Week 4

Small bonus (+50 pts). Builds the habit.

Week 8

Mid reward (free drink/item). Habit's locked in.

Week 12

Aspirational (VIP treatment, big % off). The "trophy."

How customers experience it

On the Rewards tab they tap the fire pill at the top and see a grid of cells. Filled cells (orange-red gradient) are completed weeks; milestone cells (gold gradient + ★ REWARD tag) stand out so they know "next week I get a reward." The current week's cell pulses.

Chapter 6

Memberships — three payment modes

You can sell a paid premium tier on top of your standard loyalty program — VIP membership, Inner Circle, whatever you call it. Members get perks like 2× point multiplier or priority booking. Atlas gives you three ways to collect the recurring payment.

How to set up

  1. Manager dashboard → Membership tab
  2. Membership name, monthly price, perks list (one perk per line)
  3. Choose your payment mode (see below)
  4. Toggle Enable
  5. Save

The three payment modes

Mode 1 · Default · Lowest friction

At the front desk

Customer signs up in the app → membership status flips to "pending" → they pay at your counter using any method you accept (cash, POS card, Venmo, Cash App) → staff taps Activate in the front-desk app. Zero processor setup required.

Mode 2 · Online payment

External payment link

Paste any payment URL (Square invoice, PayPal subscribe, Shopify checkout, anything). When a customer taps "Join Membership," that URL opens in a new tab. They pay there. Staff confirms the deposit then taps Activate in the front-desk app. Works with any payment processor.

Mode 3 · Full auto

Stripe (auto-activate)

Paste your Stripe secret key + webhook secret. Customer signs up in the app → redirected to Stripe Checkout → pays → webhook auto-activates them. Zero staff action needed. Best for high volume.

Activating a pending membership (Modes 1 + 2)

When a customer requests membership in modes 1 or 2, you'll see them in the Pending memberships queue on the Front-desk tab (gold-plated card — you can't miss it). Confirm their payment, then tap Activate. The customer gets a push notification: "You're a member! 🎉"

Most local businesses pick Mode 1. You already collect payment somehow. Atlas doesn't need to touch the money to track who's a member.
Chapter 7

Front-desk operations

The day-to-day surface. Your front-desk staff lives here. Switch to the Front-desk tab in the manager dashboard.

Scanning a customer's QR code

Two ways:

  • Camera scan — tap "Scan code" on the front-desk hero, point your tablet camera at the customer's QR. Atlas auto-detects the code.
  • USB scanner — plug in any cheap USB QR scanner (~$30 on Amazon). Atlas auto-listens for HID input. Best for fast-paced front desks.

Awarding points

Once a customer is scanned in:

  1. The Award Points panel opens automatically
  2. Pick a reason (visit, purchase, manual adjustment)
  3. Enter the amount
  4. Tap Award → instant balance update on the customer's phone
Made a mistake? Tap the Undo button on the same panel within 30 seconds. Reverses the award cleanly. After 30 seconds, you can still manually adjust their balance.

Manual code entry

If the camera or USB scanner isn't cooperating, tap Type code and enter the 6-character code from the customer's member card. Same result.

Fulfilling redemptions

When a customer redeems a reward, they get a 7-character code. Same scan flow — Atlas auto-detects whether you scanned a member or a redemption.

Customer search

Top of the Front-desk tab — type a customer name, phone, or email. Auto-suggests matches from your member list. Click to open their profile.

Daily recap card

The card at the top shows today's stats live: new members, points awarded, redemptions. Refresh-free — updates as the day happens.

Chapter 8

Google reviews — approve & award

Atlas turns happy customers into 5-star Google reviews on autopilot. Three steps for the customer; one tap for you.

The customer flow

  1. Customer sees a red "!" badge on the Rewards tab in the bottom nav
  2. Tap the tab → page scrolls directly to the review row, brief ring flash
  3. Tap Review on Google → opens the Google review form for your business
  4. Customer writes the review → comes back to the app → taps "I left my review"
  5. Optionally pastes the link to their review
  6. Status flips to pending (orange "!") → waiting for you to verify

Approving the review (your job)

  1. On the Front-desk tab, the Pending Google reviews queue appears (4-color Google-branded card — can't miss it)
  2. Click "View their review" to confirm it's real
  3. Tap Approve & award → points credited, customer gets a push: "Your review was verified 🎉"
  4. OR tap Reject if it's not legit (rare)

One-and-done

Once approved, that customer cannot submit another Google review for your business. The red badge disappears from their app. Prevents abuse and keeps your review velocity natural.

Setting your Google review URL

  1. Google Business Profile → Get more reviews
  2. Copy the link Google gives you
  3. Atlas → Settings → Business Settings → Google Review URL → paste
  4. Save

That's the link customers land on when they tap "Review on Google." Without it, the button shows a "not set up yet" state.

Chapter 9

Team — invite managers & front-desk

Atlas has two staff roles, and you'll use both.

Manager

Full access. Sees Insights, Billing, Team management, all settings. For owners + ops managers.

Front desk

Limited access. Only sees scan, award points, fulfill redemptions, activate memberships, approve reviews. Cannot see billing or business-level analytics.

Sending an invite

  1. Manager dashboard → Team tab
  2. Tap Invite
  3. Enter their email
  4. Pick a role (manager OR front-desk)
  5. Send → they get a magic-link email
  6. They click the link → set their password → land in the right dashboard

Removing a team member

  1. Team tab → find them in the list
  2. Click the ⋮ menu → Remove from team
  3. Confirm

They lose access immediately. Their previous actions stay logged.

Best practice: Use front-desk role for everyone unless they genuinely need billing/analytics access. Lower-privilege accounts = fewer accidents.
Chapter 10

In-app notifications + broadcasts

Atlas has two kinds of notifications: automatic (Atlas sends them for you) and manual (you broadcast them).

Automatic notifications

These fire on their own, no setup needed:

  • Streak — milestone reached, streak about to break
  • Daily check-in — confirmation when a customer checks in
  • Google review — "Your review was verified 🎉"
  • Automated offer — "A new offer just dropped"
  • Reward expiration — "Your reward expires in 48 hours"
  • Membership activation — "You're a member!"

Manual broadcasts

One-tap message to every enrolled member of your business. Holiday hours, surprise drops, anything.

  1. Manager dashboard → Notifications tab
  2. Title (max 80 chars — short + punchy)
  3. Body (max 240 chars — the details)
  4. Pick where the notification opens to (Home / Rewards / Scan / Profile)
  5. Tap Send to everyone

Every enrolled member gets both:

  • An in-app notification (bell badge updates immediately)
  • A push notification (if they've granted permission)

Push notification permissions

Customers grant push permission the first time they tap the notification bell after installing the app. Android works automatically. iOS only works if they installed via Safari (Add to Home Screen) — Chrome on iOS can't do push.

Send sparingly. Push fatigue is real. Once or twice a week max for broadcasts. Atlas's automatic notifications already create a steady cadence.

Chapter 12

Billing & manual MRR tracking

The Billing tab handles two things: your subscription to Atlas (paid to Andrew's agency) AND tracking your own customers' membership revenue.

Your subscription to Atlas

You pay Atlas's agency a fixed monthly + one-time setup. The exact amount lives in your contract with the agency. You don't manage this through the dashboard — Atlas's team handles invoicing.

Tracking customer membership revenue

When you're using front-desk or external link payment modes, Atlas doesn't see the money — you do. The Billing tab lets you log MRR + setup fees manually so your dashboard still reflects real numbers.

  1. Billing tab → click + Log MRR / setup fee
  2. Pick the customer's business membership
  3. Enter monthly amount in dollars
  4. Optionally enter setup fee
  5. Set status: Active (counts toward MRR), Pipeline / Trial (counts toward pipeline), or Canceled
  6. Save

The MRR + pipeline numbers on your Insights tab update immediately.

When to switch to Stripe automation

If your membership volume exceeds ~20-30 active customers and manually logging gets tedious, switch your membership payment mode to Stripe (Mode 3). Then:

  • Get your Stripe secret key from Stripe Dashboard → Developers → API keys
  • Paste into Membership tab → Stripe section
  • Set up a Stripe webhook pointing to your business's webhook URL (Atlas shows you the URL)
  • From now on: customer subscribes in-app → Stripe handles payment → webhook auto-activates them → MRR auto-tracks
You can mix modes per customer. Some customers prefer cash at the desk; others want Stripe auto-pay. Atlas doesn't care — just use whichever mode each customer set up under.
Chapter 13

Troubleshooting

"A customer can't sign up"

  • Check they're on your branded subdomain (not the root atlas-engine.org)
  • If iPhone: they must use Safari, not Chrome, to install the PWA
  • Have them clear cookies for your subdomain and try again

"Push notifications aren't working"

  • iPhone customers MUST install the app to home screen (Safari → Share → Add to Home Screen) BEFORE granting push permission. Pre-install permission requests fail silently.
  • Android works through Chrome directly
  • If push permission was denied, the customer has to reset it in browser site settings

"The QR scanner isn't picking up codes"

  • Make sure the printed QR is at least 3×3 inches and well-lit
  • Try the USB scanner mode — auto-detects most cheap HID scanners
  • As a fallback: tap Type code and enter the 6-character code from the customer's member card

"I can't approve a Google review"

  • Your role might be front-desk only — check with the owner
  • If the error says "permission denied," the SQL migration may not be applied — contact Atlas support
  • If you're getting "no permission" repeatedly, try signing out and back in

"Customer says they paid but their membership isn't active"

  • If you use front-desk or external link mode — go to the Pending memberships queue and tap Activate
  • If you use Stripe mode — check Stripe Dashboard for the customer's payment status; if paid, the webhook may have failed

"The app looks slow / things won't load"

  • Refresh the page (Ctrl/Cmd+R)
  • Check your internet connection
  • If a specific RPC is failing repeatedly, contact Atlas support — could be a database issue

Getting help

Atlas support: email support@atlas-engine.org or text the agency. Most issues are resolved same-day during business hours.

One last thing: Atlas works best when you use it. Don't set it up and forget it. Pick one feature each week and lean in — change the featured offer Monday, broadcast a Thursday special, check Insights every Friday. Compound interest applies.
